kheer



Kheer is a popular Indian dessert that is essentially a type of rice pudding. It's made by simmering rice (usually basmati), milk, and sugar together until the mixture thickens and the rice becomes soft and creamy. Often flavored with cardamom, saffron, and sometimes garnished with nuts like almonds, cashews, or pistachios, kheer is a versatile dish enjoyed across India and neighboring countries like Pakistan and Bangladesh. It can be served warm or chilled, and variations may include additions like raisins, rose water, or even grated coconut depending on regional preferences. Kheer is commonly prepared during festivals, celebrations, and religious occasions, embodying a rich culinary tradition that spans centuries in South Asia.

Ingredients for kheer

  • Rice: Basmati rice or any long-grain rice is commonly used. It adds texture and thickness to the kheer.
  • Milk: Full-fat milk is preferred for a rich and creamy texture.
    You can adjust the quantity based on how thick or thin you want your kheer to be.
  • Sugar: White granulated sugar is most commonly used, but
    you can adjust the amount according to your sweetness preference.
  • Cardamom: Ground cardamom powder adds a delightful aroma and flavor to the kheer.
  • Saffron: Optional, but adds a beautiful golden color and subtle flavor.
    It's usually soaked in a little warm milk before adding to the kheer.
  • Nuts: Slivered or chopped almonds, cashews, and pistachios are typically used.
    They add crunch and richness to the kheer.
  • Raisins: Optional, but some recipes include raisins for added sweetness and texture.
  • Ghee (Clarified Butter): A small amount may be used to roast the nuts before adding to the kheer for enhanced flavor.

Step to make kheer

  1. Wash and Soak Rice:
    Wash the rice under running water until the water runs clear. Soak the rice in water for about 15-20 minutes.


  2. Cook Rice:
    Drain the soaked rice and add it to a heavy-bottomed pan with milk. Bring to a boil over medium heat,
    then reduce the heat to low and simmer until the rice is fully cooked and the milk reduces and thickens.
    Stir occasionally to prevent sticking and burning.

  3. Add Sugar and Flavorings:
    Once the rice is cooked and the milk has reduced to a creamy consistency, add sugar, cardamom powder,
    and saffron (if using). Stir well until the sugar dissolves completely.

  4. Roast Nuts:
    In a separate small pan, heat a little ghee and roast the nuts until golden brown. Add the raisins and roast until they plump up.

  5. Combine and Simmer:
    Add the roasted nuts (and raisins) to the kheer and simmer for another 5-10 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the kheer thickens further to your desired consistency.

  6. Serve:
    Serve kheer warm or chilled, garnished with more nuts and a sprinkle of cardamom powder if desired.
