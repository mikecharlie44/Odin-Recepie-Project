Thakali Food


Thakali food, originating from the Mustang region of Nepal, is renowned for its simplicity, hearty flavors, and use of local ingredients. At the heart of Thakali cuisine is dal-bhat, a staple dish featuring lentil soup (dal) and steamed rice (bhat), seasoned with turmeric, cumin, and garlic. The cuisine also highlights fermented leafy greens known as gundruk, which add a tangy flavor to meals. Buckwheat plays a significant role, often seen in dishes like buckwheat pancakes (dhido) or noodles (phapar ko roti). Meat dishes such as grilled sekuwa and curried masu (chicken or goat) are popular, reflecting the region's agrarian lifestyle. Achar, spicy pickles made with mustard oil and local spices, accompany meals, adding zest to the palate. Thakali cuisine's incorporation of local herbs like timur (Sichuan pepper) and jimbu (Himalayan herb) further enriches its distinctive flavors. Lastly, desserts like apple pie, made from the region's abundant apple orchards, provide a sweet conclusion to a meal deeply rooted in tradition and local culture.

Ingredients for Thakali

  • Rice: Basmati or other varieties are used as a staple.
  • Dal (Lentils): Various lentils such as yellow lentils (moong dal), black lentils (urad dal), or red lentils (masoor dal) are common
  • Meat: Especially goat meat (mutton) and occasionally chicken.
  • Vegetables: Including potatoes, spinach, tomatoes, cauliflower, green beans, and bell peppers.
  • Spices and Herbs: Turmeric, cumin, coriander, mustard seeds, fenugreek, garlic, ginger, and fresh cilantro.
  • Dairy: Ghee (clarified butter), yogurt, and occasionally milk in desserts.

Step to make Thakali

  1. Prepare the Ingredients:
    Rice:
    Rinse the rice thoroughly and soak if required, then cook it using your preferred method (boiling, steaming, etc.).

    Dal (Lentils): Rinse the lentils (such as yellow lentils or red lentils) and cook them with water, turmeric, and salt until soft.
    You can temper the dal with spices like cumin seeds, mustard seeds, and garlic for added flavor.

    Meat:
    If using mutton or chicken, marinate the meat with spices such as turmeric, cumin, coriander, and ginger-garlic paste.
    Cook the meat until tender either in a pressure cooker, slow cooker, or on the stove.

    Vegetables:
    Prepare vegetables like potatoes, spinach, tomatoes, cauliflower, and beans by washing and chopping them as needed.

  2. Prepare the Spice Mixtures:
    Thakali cuisine often uses a blend of spices and herbs. Common spice mixtures may include a combination of ground coriander,
    cumin, turmeric, and occasionally mustard seeds or fenugreek seeds. These spices can be dry-roasted and ground for a richer flavor.

  3. Cooking Process:
    Place a small amount of filling (about 1 teaspoon) in the center of each wrapper.
    Dip your finger in water and moisten the edge of the wrapper to help seal the dumpling.

  4. Serving:
    Fold the wrapper over the filling to create a half-moon shape. Pinch the edges together
    firmly to seal the dumpling. You can use pleats or simply press the edges together for a secure seal.
