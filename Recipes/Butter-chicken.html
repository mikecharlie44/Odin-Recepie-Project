Butter chicken



Butter chicken, also known as Murgh Makhani, is a popular North Indian dish renowned for its rich, creamy texture and flavorful taste.
Originating from Delhi, India, it consists of tender chicken pieces marinated in yogurt and a blend of spices such as
garam masala, ginger, garlic, and chili powder. The chicken is traditionally cooked in a tandoor (clay oven) for an authentic smoky flavor,
although modern adaptations use a regular oven or grill. The sauce, or makhani gravy, is made with butter, tomatoes, cream, and various spices
like fenugreek (kasuri methi) for a distinctive taste. Butter chicken is often enjoyed with naan bread or rice, making it a favorite in Indian restaurants worldwide for its creamy, mildly spicy, and comforting flavors.


Ingredients for Butter-chicken

  • Chicken: Usually boneless, skinless chicken thighs or breasts, cut into bite-sized pieces.
  • Marinade:Yogurt: Plain yogurt helps tenderize the chicken.
    Ginger-garlic paste: A combination of finely minced ginger and garlic.
    Lemon juice: Adds brightness and helps in tenderizing the chicken.
    Kashmiri red chili powder: Provides color and mild heat.
    Salt: To season the chicken.
  • Butter Chicken Sauce: Tomato Puree or Sauce: Smooth tomato puree or sauce forms the base of the sauce.
    Butter: Generous amounts are used for richness and flavor.
    Cream: Heavy cream or full-fat cream for a creamy texture.
    Spices: Garam masala, cumin powder, coriander powder, turmeric powder, and Kashmiri red chili powder for flavor and color.
    Kasuri Methi (Dried Fenugreek Leaves): Adds a distinct flavor and aroma to the sauce.
    Sugar: Balances the acidity of tomatoes.
  • Additional Ingredients:Onion: Finely chopped or pureed. Cashew Paste: Optional for added creaminess and richness.
    Fresh Ginger: Finely minced or grated. Green Chilies: For heat, finely chopped (optional).
    Coriander Leaves (Cilantro): Chopped, for garnish.

Step to make Butter-chicken

  1. Marinate the Chicken:
    In a bowl, mix together yogurt, ginger-garlic paste, lemon juice, Kashmiri red chili powder, and salt. Add chicken pieces and coat well.
    Marinate for at least 30 minutes, preferably longer.


  2. Prepare the Sauce:
    Heat butter in a pan. Add finely chopped onion and sauté until translucent. Add ginger-garlic paste
    and green chilies (if using), sauté until fragrant Add tomato puree or sauce and cook until the
    raw smell of tomatoes goes away and oil starts to separate from the mixture.
    Add spices (garam masala, cumin powder, coriander powder, turmeric powder, Kashmiri red chili powder) and cook for another minute.

  3. Cook the Chicken:
    Add the marinated chicken along with the marinade into the sauce. Cook on medium heat until the chicken is cooked
    through and the sauce thickens, stirring occasionally. This usually takes about 15-20 minutes.

  4. Finish the Dish:
    Add cream and kasuri methi (crushed between palms), stir well. Adjust salt and sugar according to taste.
    Let it simmer for a couple of minutes until the flavors meld together.
    Garnish with chopped coriander leaves (cilantro) and a drizzle of cream if desired.

  5. Serve:
    Serve butter chicken hot with naan bread, roti, or steamed basmati rice.
